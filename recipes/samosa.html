Classic Potato & Pea Samosas with Vibrant Green Sauce

A cornerstone of traditional Indian street food, the samosa is a masterclass in texture and flavor contrast. This beloved dish features a beautifully flaky, crisp pastry pyramid packed with a deeply savory, hand-mashed filling of spiced potatoes, green peas, ginger, and aromatic garam masala.

Paired alongside is a zesty, refreshing green chutney crafted from fresh cilantro, mint, and a touch of lemon juice. The cooling, herbaceous notes of the sauce perfectly cut through the rich, comforting warmth of the hot, fried pastry.

Part 1: Classic Potato & Pea Samosas

Ingredients

Pastry Shell (Dough)

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ajwain seeds (carom seeds) – optional but traditional
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 4–6 tablespoons ice-cold water

For the Samosa Filling

  • 3 large potatoes (boiled, peeled, and roughly mashed)
  • ½ cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1-inch piece ginger (finely grated)
  • 1–2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur) or lemon juice
  • Handful of fresh cilantro (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Preparation Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, and ajwain seeds.

  2. Add the 4 tablespoons of oil or ghee. Rub the flour between your fingers until it resembles coarse breadcrumbs. When you squeeze some flour in your palm, it should hold its shape. (This step is crucial for a flaky crust).

  3. Gradually add the cold water, one tablespoon at a time, and knead into a firm, smooth dough. Do not over-knead.

  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.

  5. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies; sauté for 1 minute.

  6. Add the mashed potatoes and green peas.

  7. Stir in all the powdered spices (turmeric, coriander, garam masala, dry mango powder) and salt. Mix well and cook on medium heat for 3–4 minutes, mashing any large potato chunks.

  8. Turn off the heat and mix in the fresh cilantro. Let the filling cool completely before using.

  9. After the dough has rested, divide it into 5 or 6 equal portions and roll them into smooth balls.

  10. Roll each ball into an oval or round shape (about 6-7 inches in diameter). The dough should not be too thin.

  11. Cut the rolled circle in half with a knife to get two semi-circles.

  12. Take one semi-circle. Apply water along the straight edge. Fold the straight edge over to form a cone, overlapping the edges slightly. Press the seam firmly to seal.

  13. Hold the cone in your hand. Stuff it with 2–3 tablespoons of the cooled potato filling.

  14. Apply water to the inner circumference of the cone's open edge. Make a small pleat on the back edge (this helps the samosa stand) and press the front and back edges firmly together. Ensure there are no gaps.

  15. Heat oil for deep frying in a wok or deep pan over medium-low heat.

  16. Drop a tiny piece of dough into the oil. It should slowly rise to the top with small bubbles. If it rises instantly, the oil is too hot.

  17. Carefully drop 3–4 samosas into the oil (depending on the pan size).

  18. Fry on low to medium heat for 10–12 minutes. This slow process ensures a crispy exterior.

  19. Once the samosas are a deep golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels.

Part 2: Vibrant Green Sauce (Cilantro-Mint Chutney)

Ingredients

  • 1 large bunch fresh cilantro (stems are okay)
  • ½ bunch fresh mint leaves (leaves only)
  • 1–2 green chilies (adjust for spice level)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (peeled)
  • 1 tablespoon roasted chana dal or 1 tablespoon chopped cashews (for creaminess/body)
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon black salt (kala namak) or regular salt
  • ¼ teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1–2 tablespoons ice-cold water (or a few ice cubes) – This helps maintain the bright green color.

Preparation Steps

  1. Place the cilantro, mint leaves, green chilies, ginger, and roasted chana dal (or cashews) in a blender.

  2. Add the lemon juice, black salt, and cumin powder.

  3. Add 1 tablespoon of ice-cold water and blend to a smooth paste. Add more water only if necessary to get the blender moving. The chutney should be thick, not watery.

  4. Transfer to a serving bowl. This chutney stays fresh in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
